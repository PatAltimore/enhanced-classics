The darkness was thick and palpable, like a veil drawn tightly across my senses. I stood in the cavernous depths of the Morlocks’ domain, the **air heavy with damp** and faintly metallic odors that clung to my skin and clothes. The faint hum of machinery echoed distantly, a strange and mechanical heartbeat that pulsed through the labyrinthine tunnels. My lantern flickered weakly, its flame a frail beacon against the oppressive gloom. Around me, shadows stirred and shifted, and I knew that I was not alone.

The Morlocks were near. I could feel them—hear them, even—though they kept themselves just out of sight. Their low, guttural sounds reverberated in the darkness, accompanied now and then by the faint patter of their bare feet against the stone. It was a disquieting symphony of presence, a constant reminder that I had trespassed into their world. Yet there was no turning back. Somewhere in this subterranean maze lay my **Time Machine**, stolen and hidden away by these troglodytic beings.

I pressed forward, gripping the handle of the lantern with white-knuckled intensity. The feeble light revealed little more than jagged walls and rusted pipes, the remnants of a bygone age. The tunnels seemed endless, sprawling in all directions like the roots of some ancient, malevolent tree. I could only hope that my path would lead me to the machine before the Morlocks made their move.

As I walked, I reflected on the strange **juxtaposition of worlds** I had encountered in this distant future. Abo
